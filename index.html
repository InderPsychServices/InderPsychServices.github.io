How It Works

From Referral to
Delivered Report

A streamlined, attorney-first process designed to minimize administrative burden and maximize report quality.

1
Attorney Referral
Contact Mr. Singh directly by phone, text, or email with the client's name and evaluation type required. All attorney inquiries receive a same-day response — without exception.
2
Intake & Scheduling
The client completes a brief intake. The evaluation is scheduled via a secure HIPAA-compliant telehealth link at the client's convenience. A 50% deposit is collected at scheduling; the balance is due prior to report release.
3
Clinical Evaluation
Mr. Singh conducts a comprehensive one-on-one clinical interview in the client's preferred language — directly, without an interpreter when Punjabi or Hindi is spoken. Validated psychometric instruments are administered and scored. Sessions typically run 60 to 90 minutes.
4
Report Preparation
Mr. Singh synthesizes interview findings, psychometric data, and clinical history into a complete forensic report. Every report is DSM-5 aligned, written in plain legal language, and reviewed personally before release.
5
Delivery & Follow-Up
The completed report is delivered securely to the referring attorney. Addendum services, supplemental documentation, and expert consultation are available upon request.

An immigration psychological evaluation is a forensic mental health assessment conducted by a licensed clinician to document the psychological impact of trauma, persecution, domestic violence, or family separation on an individual involved in immigration proceedings. These evaluations are central to establishing the mental health state of the applicant, describing the hardships experienced, and articulating the long-term psychological consequences relevant to their legal matter. Mr. Singh's evaluations are DSM-5 aligned and written to meet the evidentiary standards of immigration courts and USCIS adjudicators.
